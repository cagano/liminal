# Stack Selection Criteria

The filter for deciding whether an idea earns a place in the personal cognitive compounding stack.

Source: crystallized from `.agents/explorations/liminal-framework.md` through direct user articulation. `[VALIDATED]` — lived and tested, not hypothetical.

---

## The Filter

An idea is stack-eligible if it passes all four tests:

| Test | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Cost gate** | Is the entry cost low enough that failure is survivable? |
| **Dimension count** | Does it hit 2+ of: wealth, health, time, character? |
| **Stack fit** | Does it amplify something already in the stack? |
| **Permanence** | Is the gain ongoing, not one-time? |

---

## Core Principle: Multi-Dimensional Leverage

A stack-eligible idea hits more than one dimension simultaneously. Single-dimension gains are additive. Multi-dimension gains are multiplicative — that's what makes them exponential.

**Example — treadmill desk:**
- Sedentary problem → solved (health)
- Exercise time scarcity → solved (time)
- Already coding anyway → no additional time cost
- One purchase → permanent ongoing gain

Three gains, one move, low entry cost. Stack-eligible.

---

## The Dimension Map

| Dimension | What counts |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Wealth** | Reduces expenses, increases income, builds assets |
| **Health** | Physical, cognitive, longevity |
| **Time** | Frees time, eliminates tasks, increases throughput |
| **Character** | Builds discipline, sharpens thinking, compounds identity |

---

## The Cost Gate Logic

"Cheap" is not just about money — it's about **asymmetric risk**. Low cost of entry means:
- Failure is survivable and reversible
- No large bet required before signal emerges
- Can be tried fast and cut fast if it doesn't compound

This is the filter that makes the stack maintainable. Without it, every interesting idea becomes a candidate and the stack bloats.

## The Pareto Ingestion Rule

When ingesting any material (book, course, framework), extract the 20% that carries 80% of the impact. The stack is built from these extractions — not from full ingestion of everything.

Operationally: use NotebookLM podcasts + AI conversations to surface the high-leverage fraction before deciding whether to go deeper.
